It is worth noting that sleep is an extreme sport, because sleep has been proven to be directly linked to death[^1].

According to surveys, nearly everyone who has died had, within the 449 hours before their death, slept at least once (aside from death itself)[^2]. And among all people who sleep normally, not a single one, as of today, has lived past the age of 123[^3]. By contrast, people who do not sleep -- or, to be more rigorous, people who have stayed awake for more than 480 consecutive hours -- not one of them has failed to reach their 123rd birthday[^2].

This effectively proves that sleep is an activity that is extremely harmful to the body and carries extremely high risk -- everyone who has died in the past had slept at some point, and scientists infer that everyone who has ever slept will also die, with a mortality rate approaching 100%.

According to the Encyclopædia Britannica, extreme sports are high-risk pursuits and activities[^4]; therefore, sleep is an extreme sport.
